## Context

An independent production studio asked me to fully redesign its visual identity. The old guidelines, inherited from the studio's founding, lacked coherence: three logos coexisted, typography varied across media, and no usage rules were documented. The goal was to build a modular system able to cover both print and digital.

## Challenge

The real challenge was not drawing a beautiful logo, but designing a system that would outlive my involvement. The client needed to extend the identity to new media on their own, without calling me back. That meant clear rules, a library of reusable components, and documentation accessible to non-designers.

## Process

I started with a visual audit of the existing assets and a positioning workshop with the team. From there I defined a typographic grid based on a 1.25-ratio modular scale, then a restrained palette of five tints with variations. The logo was rethought as a flexible brand system rather than a fixed mark.

<Callout variant="note" title="Method">
  Every Figma component is documented with its margins, variants, and an example of forbidden usage — an explicit constraint beats an implicit rule.
</Callout>

I delivered everything as a shared Figma library, accompanied by a 24-page brand guide exported from InDesign. The icons and colour set were designed to stay legible in greyscale, guaranteeing accessibility across all media.

## Outcome

The studio now has a coherent identity applied across its cards, website, and email signatures. The brand guide cut roughly 70% of the back-and-forth on new materials, as the team is now self-sufficient for most variations. The system keeps evolving in their hands, which was exactly the original intent.
