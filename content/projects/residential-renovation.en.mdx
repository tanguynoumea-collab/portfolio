## Context

This project covered the heavy renovation of a 200 m² town house dating from the 1930s. The owner wanted to open up the living spaces while preserving the heritage elements of the façade. My role was to produce a complete BIM model serving as a reference for every trade, from demolition to handover.

## Challenge

The old structure held its share of surprises: undocumented load-bearing walls, uneven floor levels, and ageing networks to map. The challenge was to faithfully model the existing building from partial surveys, then overlay the new design while detecting clashes before construction rather than discovering them on site.

## Process

I first reconstructed the existing building in ArchiCAD from a laser survey and photographs. This "as-built" model became the foundation for the renovation design, modelled in parallel to compare both states.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Coordination">
  Clash detection between the new ventilation network and the existing beams revealed three major collisions — all resolved on the model before construction began.
</Callout>

I then extracted construction drawings, sections, and quantities directly from the model, which guaranteed consistency across every document. Lumion renders let the owner validate the layout and material choices before any order, reducing the risk of late changes.

## Outcome

The BIM model served as the single reference throughout the project, limiting coordination surprises. Early clash detection saved roughly three weeks of on-site rework. The owner could visualise the result thanks to the renders, and handover happened with no major deviation from the model. The project illustrates the value of a faithful as-built model.
